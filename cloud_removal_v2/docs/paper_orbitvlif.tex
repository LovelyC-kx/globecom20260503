\begin{abstract}
Space Computing Power Networks (Space-CPN) demand on-orbit data
processing within tight power envelopes, while optical cloud
removal---an essential preprocessing primitive for remote-sensing
applications---remains dominated by computation-heavy artificial
neural networks (ANNs) ill-suited to satellite power budgets.
We present \OrbitVLIF, \emph{the first end-to-end decentralized
federated learning system for satellite-onboard spiking cloud
removal}. At the system level we deploy the model on a
$5\!\times\!10$ Walker-Star constellation and aggregate via
\textsc{FedBN}\,+\,Gossip with source-level Dirichlet non-IID
partition over the joint CUHK-CR1\,+\,CR2 cloud benchmark; the
gossip-based decentralized aggregation pipeline is the core
contribution.  At the model level we adopt and integrate
state-of-the-art SNN building blocks---a dual-frequency residual
block, a frequency-hybrid spike attention module, a 5-level
quantized spike activation, and an adaptive gated skip-fusion---into a
spiking U-Net specialised for the cloud-removal task; we present
two configurations: a $14$M-parameter \emph{full-capacity} variant
that reaches SOTA-class quality on the CUHK-CR benchmarks, and a
$2$M-parameter \emph{on-orbit deployment} variant used for the
federated experiments. On a single 4090, the deployment variant
attains $25.37$\,dB / $0.7728$ PSNR/SSIM on CUHK-CR1
($+0.50$\,dB over a parameter-matched ANN U-Net at $0.57\!\times\!$
the parameter count) and $23.20$\,dB on CUHK-CR2.  Per-image
inference energy is \emph{estimated} (not hardware-measured) under
a transparent dual bound: $1.21\!\times\!$ as a conservative upper
bound that charges every non-zero MAC at the full ANN rate, and
$72\!\times\!$ as the deployment-target lower bound on
in-memory-computing substrates.
\end{abstract}

\begin{IEEEkeywords}
Space computing power networks, spiking neural networks, optical
cloud removal, decentralized federated learning, neuromorphic
computing.
\end{IEEEkeywords}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

\IEEEPARstart{O}{ptical} Earth-observation satellites in low-Earth
orbit (LEO) capture hundreds of gigabytes of imagery per orbit,
underpinning environmental monitoring, disaster response, and
defense applications. A substantial fraction of every captured
frame is, however, occluded by clouds and must be restored before
downstream analytics. Conventional pipelines downlink raw data to
ground stations for restoration, but the satellite-to-ground link
is bottlenecked by short visible windows ($\sim$10\,min per pass for
LEO) and narrow-band radio frequency channels, incurring multi-minute
end-to-end latency that defeats latency-critical applications such
as wildfire localisation~\cite{ref:earthobs1,ref:earthobs2}.

To bypass the downlink bottleneck, recent proposals on
\emph{Space Computing Power Networks} (Space-CPN) push computation
on-board, treating each orbital plane as a mesh of compute nodes
interconnected by inter-satellite laser links
(ISLs)~\cite{ref:spacecpn1,ref:spacecpn2}. Optical cloud removal
becomes a canonical Space-CPN primitive: it is needed by every
downstream task, and its data is local to individual satellites.

\textbf{Yet the on-board energy budget is severe.} LEO platforms
draw $\sim$100--200\,W from solar arrays charged during sunlit arcs
and depleted on eclipse; dense artificial neural network (ANN)
inference and training compete with attitude control, payload, and
downlink for this shared budget. On 45\,nm CMOS, multiply-accumulate
(MAC) operations dominate ANN cost at $\sim$4.6\,pJ each, which is
prohibitive for the multi-G\,MAC convolutional or transformer
backbones used by recent cloud-removal SOTA~\cite{ref:cuhkcr,
ref:darkir}.

\textbf{Spiking neural networks (SNNs) offer a structurally
different operating point.} Activations are encoded as sparse,
event-driven binary or multi-level spikes; silent neurons skip the
multiply step entirely, and a 45\,nm accumulate-only operation
costs $\sim$0.9\,pJ~\cite{ref:horowitz}. SNNs have been shown to
match ANN accuracy within $\sim$3\,\% on classification benchmarks at
roughly $10\!\times$ lower per-layer energy~\cite{ref:flsnn}. Recent
work has applied SNNs to image deraining~\cite{ref:esdnet} and
classification in satellite federated learning~\cite{ref:flsnn},
yet \emph{pixel-level cloud removal under decentralized on-orbit
training has not been demonstrated}.

The closest precedent, the FLSNN framework of Yang \emph{et al.}~\cite{ref:flsnn},
established the feasibility of decentralized federated SNN training
on a Walker-Star constellation for the EuroSAT classification task.
Three concrete gaps prevent its direct application to operational
on-orbit cloud removal: (i) \emph{task-type gap} --- pixel
regression has different gradient structure (dense, low-frequency,
residual-dominated) from logit-driven classification; (ii)
\emph{architectural gap} --- FLSNN's small spiking-CNN /
spiking-ResNet backbones lack the U-Net structure, frequency-aware
attention, and multi-level activation that recent SNN
image-restoration work~\cite{ref:fsta-snn,ref:darkir} has shown
essential for high-fidelity reconstruction; (iii)
\emph{non-IID gap} --- satellite data heterogeneity is naturally
driven by source (sensor, atmosphere, look angle) rather than by
class label, calling for a source-level partition formulation that
is absent from FLSNN's class-Dirichlet protocol.

To close these three gaps, we present \OrbitVLIF, a co-designed
spiking image-restoration network and decentralized federated
learning system tailored for on-orbit cloud removal. Our
\textbf{contributions} are:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{First end-to-end SNN-based decentralized federated
  cloud-removal system.}\;\;
  We deploy \OrbitVLIF{} on a $5\!\times\!10$ Walker-Star
  constellation and aggregate weights via \textsc{FedBN} combined
  with \emph{Gossip averaging} over inter-orbital links, under a
  source-level Dirichlet non-IID partition of the joint
  CUHK-CR1\,+\,CR2~\cite{ref:cuhkcr} benchmark. To our knowledge
  this is the first end-to-end demonstration of decentralized
  federated learning for \emph{spiking image regression} on a
  satellite constellation; the gossip-based aggregation pipeline,
  including the inter-/intra-plane communication scheduling and
  the FedBN-aware mixing rule, is the central technical
  contribution of this work.

\item \textbf{Dual-configuration model: 14\,M full-capacity SOTA
  + 2\,M on-orbit deployment.}\;\;
  We instantiate the \OrbitVLIF{} U-Net at two parameter budgets
  on a shared dataflow.  The $14$M-parameter full-capacity variant
  is benchmarked against representative cloud-removal baselines on
  CUHK-CR1\,/\,CR2 and reaches SOTA-class quality
  (Section~\ref{sec:exp-fullsota}), while the $2$M-parameter
  on-orbit deployment variant matches the energy and bandwidth
  envelope of LEO satellites and is the workhorse of the
  centralized, ablation, and federated experiments.

\item \textbf{Integration of state-of-the-art SNN building
  blocks.}\;\;
  Within the U-Net we integrate ideas from prior SNN
  image-restoration and spiking-quantization
  work~\cite{ref:fsta-snn, ref:esdnet, ref:darkir}, adapting them
  to the cloud-removal task: a dual-frequency residual block
  (\DFRB), a spectral-hybrid attention module (\SHAM), a
  $5$-level quantized spike activation (\FQS), an adaptive gated
  skip-fusion (\AGFM), and an outer-stage spectro-temporal hyper
  block (\SSHB). Module-level ablation
  (Section~\ref{sec:exp-ablation}) shows that
  this integration is co-designed: removing any single component
  drops below the parameter-matched plain ANN baseline.

\item \textbf{Transparent dual-bound energy estimation.}\;\;
  We disclose per-image inference energy with a
  literature-anchored dual bound that explicitly brackets the
  multi-level $\FQS$ quantisation: a conservative upper bound at
  the ANN MAC formula ($4.6$\,pJ/MAC, Horowitz 2014) and a
  deployment-target lower bound at the neuromorphic SOP formula
  ($77$\,fJ/SOP, used by FLSNN / ESDNet / VLIF), measuring
  $1.21\!\times\!$\,--\,$72\!\times\!$ reduction over the ANN
  baseline on CUHK-CR1.
\end{itemize}

The remainder of the paper is organized as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:sysmodel} formalises the constellation, federated
learning problem, and source-level Dirichlet non-IID partition.
Section~\ref{sec:framework} details the \OrbitVLIF{} architecture
and decentralized training pipeline. Section~\ref{sec:experiments}
reports centralized, ablation, federated, and energy
results. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.

\section{System Model}\label{sec:sysmodel}

\subsection{LEO Constellation and Inter-Satellite Links}\label{sec:sysmodel-constellation}

We consider a Walker-Star LEO constellation
\[
\mathcal{S} \;=\; \big\{\, s_{i,k} \,:\, i \in \mathcal{N},\, k \in \mathcal{K}_i \,\big\},
\]
comprising $N \!=\! |\mathcal{N}|$ orbital planes with $K \!=\!
|\mathcal{K}_i|$ satellites uniformly distributed in each plane. Each
satellite $s_{i,k}$ is equipped with two types of inter-satellite
laser links (ISLs)~\cite{ref:flsnn}: \emph{intra-plane} links connect
adjacent satellites of the same plane and are quasi-stationary
across orbital periods, supporting reliable ring-style aggregation;
\emph{inter-plane} links connect satellites of distinct planes and
are time-varying due to differential orbital motion, with
feasibility bounded by the Doppler shift. Following the standard
configuration in~\cite{ref:flsnn} we instantiate $N \!=\! 5$, $K \!=\!
10$, yielding $NK \!=\! 50$ satellites and a fixed inter-plane
chain topology.

\subsection{Federated Cloud-Removal Problem}\label{sec:sysmodel-problem}

Each satellite $s_{i,k}$ holds a local dataset
$\D_{i,k} = \{(\mathbf{c}_n, \mathbf{y}_n)\}$ of paired
\mbox{(cloudy,\,clear)} image tiles, with $\mathbf{c}_n,
\mathbf{y}_n \in \R^{3 \times H \times W}$. Let $g_{\mathbf{x}}\colon
\R^{3\times H\times W}\!\to\!\R^{3\times H\times W}$ denote the
cloud-removal network parameterised by $\mathbf{x} \in \R^d$ (with
$d \!\approx\! 2.30\!\times\!10^6$ for \OrbitVLIF), and let
$\ell\!:\R^{3\times H\times W}\!\times\!\R^{3\times H\times
W}\!\to\!\R_{\geq 0}$ be a pixel-level reconstruction loss
(Section~\ref{sec:framework-loss}). The federation jointly minimises
the global empirical risk
\begin{align}
\min_{\mathbf{x}\in\R^d}\;\;
f(\mathbf{x}) \;&=\; \frac{1}{NK}\!\sum_{i\in\mathcal{N}}
                       \sum_{k\in\mathcal{K}_i}\!
                       f_{i,k}(\mathbf{x}), \label{eq:global-obj}\\
\text{where}\quad
f_{i,k}(\mathbf{x}) \;&=\; \frac{1}{|\D_{i,k}|}
                          \!\sum_{(\mathbf{c},\mathbf{y})\in\D_{i,k}}
                          \!\ell\!\big(g_{\mathbf{x}}(\mathbf{c}),\,\mathbf{y}\big)
\label{eq:local-loss}
\end{align}
is the local empirical risk on satellite $s_{i,k}$. The centralised
solution requires raw imagery to be downlinked, which is precluded
by the bottleneck described in Section~\ref{sec:intro}; we therefore
adopt fully on-orbit decentralised training.

\subsection{Decentralised Learning Procedure}\label{sec:sysmodel-procedure}

Each global round $t \in \{0,\ldots,T\!-\!1\}$ proceeds in four
steps:

\textbf{(S1) Local update.} Every satellite performs $E$ epochs of
local AdamW on its dataset starting from the round-$t$ broadcast
weights $\mathbf{x}^{(t)}$:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,e+1)} \;=\;
\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,e)} \;-\;
\eta\,\nabla\widetilde f_{i,k}\!\big(\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,e)}\big),
\quad e\in\{0,\ldots,E\!-\!1\}, \label{eq:local-sgd}
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde f_{i,k}$ is the mini-batch stochastic estimate of
$f_{i,k}$, $\eta$ is the cosine-annealed learning rate, and
$\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,0)} \!=\! \mathbf{x}^{(t)}$.

\textbf{(S2) Intra-plane ring AllReduce.} Within each orbital plane
$i$, the $K$ satellites pool their post-update weights through a
ring AllReduce primitive over the quasi-stationary intra-plane
ISLs to obtain the per-plane consensus
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{x}_i^{(t+\tfrac12)} \;=\;
\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k\in\mathcal{K}_i}\!\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,E)}.
\label{eq:intra-plane-avg}
\end{equation}

\textbf{(S3) Inter-plane Gossip aggregation.} The $N$ plane-level
models are mixed across the inter-plane chain topology by a
single-step Gossip averaging with mixing matrix
$\mathbf{W}\in\R^{N\times N}$ (doubly stochastic, $\mathbf{W}^\top\!=\!\mathbf{W}$),
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{x}^{(t+1)}_{i} \;=\;
\sum_{j\in\mathcal{N}}\!W_{ij}\,\mathbf{x}_j^{(t+\tfrac12)},
\quad i\in\mathcal{N}.
\label{eq:gossip}
\end{equation}
Following \textsc{FedBN}~\cite{ref:fedbn}, the BN affine and running
statistics are excluded from~\eqref{eq:gossip} and kept local to
each plane; convolutional weights and biases participate normally.

\textbf{(S4) Broadcast.} The aggregated plane-level model
$\mathbf{x}^{(t+1)}_i$ is re-broadcast to every satellite
$s_{i,k}$, completing round $t$.

\subsection{Source-Level Dirichlet Non-IID Partition}
\label{sec:sysmodel-partition}

To capture the natural feature-level heterogeneity of satellite
imagery, we partition the joint training pool over
$S \!=\! 2$ source labels (CUHK-CR1 thin clouds and CUHK-CR2 thick
clouds; see Section~\ref{sec:exp-setup}). For each satellite
$s_{i,k}$ we draw a per-source mixing vector
\begin{equation}
\boldsymbol{p}_{i,k} \;=\; (p_{i,k}^{(1)},\,p_{i,k}^{(2)})
\;\sim\;\Dir(\alpha\!\cdot\!\mathbf{1}_S),
\label{eq:dirichlet}
\end{equation}
and sample tiles from each source proportional to
$p_{i,k}^{(s)}$ subject to a minimum of five tiles per satellite.
Smaller $\alpha$ produces more concentrated single-source clients;
we adopt $\alpha \!=\! 0.1$ as the default, which yields a regime
in which $30$ of the $50$ satellites ($60\,\%$) hold the floor of
five tiles while three satellites individually hold over a hundred
tiles each (the actual partition log records sizes
$\{117,118,118\}$). Sensitivity to $\alpha$ is examined in
Section~\ref{sec:exp-federated}.

\section{The \OrbitVLIF{} Framework}\label{sec:framework}

The \OrbitVLIF{} framework consists of two co-designed parts: a
quantized spiking U-Net (Section~\ref{sec:framework-arch}) and a
decentralized federated training pipeline
(Section~\ref{sec:framework-fl}). Figure~\ref{fig:overview} shows
the full system: the network ingests a 4-D RGB tensor
$\mathbf{c}\!\in\!\R^{B\times 3\times H\times W}$ on each satellite,
produces a clean image $\hat{\mathbf{y}}$ via four \OrbitVLIF{}
building blocks (Fig.~\ref{fig:modules}), and shares
weights across $50$ satellites under \textsc{FedBN}\,+\,Gossip.

\subsection{\OrbitVLIF{} Architecture}\label{sec:framework-arch}

\OrbitVLIF{} is built atop a three-level spiking U-Net skeleton
(Fig.~\ref{fig:overview}\,(a)).
The shallow level $\ell\!=\!0$ keeps the full $H\!\times\!W$
spatial resolution and uses a single \emph{Spectro-temporal
Spike Hyper-Block} (\SSHB) at channel width $C$;
the two deeper levels $\ell\!\in\!\{1,2\}$ downsample to
$H/2^{\ell}$ via PixelUnshuffle and use \DFRB{} blocks at channel
widths $\{2C,4C\}$ with block counts $\{2,4\}$.
There is \emph{no separate bottleneck}: the deepest encoder feature
($\ell\!=\!2$, $H/4$ resolution, $4C$ channels) flows directly
into the symmetric decoder through a wide-channel U-bend, and the
decoder uses \DFRB{} block counts $\{2,2\}$ at the two coarse
stages followed by \emph{two sequential} \SSHB{} blocks at the
final $H\!\times\!W$ stage.
Skip connections at the two outer levels ($\ell\!\in\!\{0,1\}$)
pass through \AGFM{}; the deepest level has no separate skip
because the direct U-bend already transmits the full $4C$-channel
encoder state.
A global residual is added on the network output:
\begin{equation}
\hat{\mathbf{y}} \;=\; \mathbf{c} \;+\;
\mathrm{Conv}\!\Big(\,\overline{\mathrm{Decoder}\!\big(\mathrm{Encoder}(\mathbf{c})\big)}^{(T)}\Big),
\label{eq:globalresidual}
\end{equation}
where $\overline{(\cdot)}^{(T)}$ denotes the temporal mean over the
$T\!=\!4$ spike-train steps used by every spiking module. Setting
setting $C\!=\!24$ yields $d \approx 2.30\,$M trainable parameters
(deployment variant used throughout the federated experiments),
while $C\!=\!56$ yields $d \approx 14$M (full-capacity SOTA variant
benchmarked in Section~\ref{sec:exp-fullsota}); both share the
same dataflow.

In the rest of this section we describe the five building blocks
used inside the U-Net.  Throughout the design \emph{we integrate
ideas from prior SNN image-restoration and spiking-quantization
work}~\cite{ref:fsta-snn, ref:esdnet, ref:darkir} rather than
introduce them from scratch; our contribution at the model level
is the cloud-removal-specific dataflow that combines them under a
shared on-orbit energy budget.

\subsubsection{\FQS{}: 5-Level Quantized Spike}\label{sec:framework-5qs}

\textbf{Problem.}\;\;
Binary LIF activations $s_t\!\in\!\{0,1\}$ enable accumulate-only
arithmetic but compress the per-step dynamic range to a single bit,
which we find insufficient for pixel-level regression where
intermediate features must encode subtle texture and colour
gradients. \textbf{Candidate.}\;\;Continuous-valued ANN-style ReLU
restores dynamic range but eliminates the energy advantage. We
seek a third operating point that retains spike sparsity and yet
expresses gradient magnitude.

\textbf{Our choice.}\;\;
Given the integrated membrane potential $u_t$ at time-step $t$, the
$\FQS$ activation outputs a $5$-level quantized spike
\begin{equation}
s_t \;=\; \tfrac{1}{4}\!\cdot\!\mathrm{round}\!\big(\,\mathrm{clip}(u_t,\,0,\,4)\,\big)
\;\in\;\{0,\,\tfrac14,\,\tfrac12,\,\tfrac34,\,1\},
\label{eq:5qs-forward}
\end{equation}
combined with the soft-reset
$u_{t+1}\!=\!\beta\,u_t\!+\!I_t\!-\!s_t\!\cdot\!V_{\mathrm{th}}$,
where $\beta$ is the leak factor and $V_{\mathrm{th}}\!=\!0.15$ the
threshold. \textbf{Improvement.}\;\;
The non-differentiable~\eqref{eq:5qs-forward} is paired with a
rectangular-window surrogate gradient
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial s_t}{\partial u_t}\;\approx\;
\frac{1}{4}\!\cdot\!\mathbb{1}\{0\le u_t\le 4\},
\label{eq:5qs-surrogate}
\end{equation}
matching the active range of the forward step function and yielding
unit-area gradient flow. Empirically (Section~\ref{sec:exp-ablation})
$\FQS$ improves PSNR by $0.30$\,dB over the binary-spike ablation
under matched training.

\subsubsection{\DFRB{}: Dual-Frequency Residual Block}\label{sec:framework-dfrb}

\textbf{Problem.}\;\;
A single LIF non-linearity acts as a temporal high-pass filter:
when the membrane potential rises above $V_{\mathrm{th}}$, the
neuron fires a spike that captures rapid temporal change but
discards spatial high-frequency content carried by the residual
$\mathbf{x}\!-\!\mathbf{x}_h$. For pixel-level cloud removal, both
temporal contour cues (cloud edge transitions) and spatial
high-frequency texture (vegetation, urban grid) are
information-bearing, and a single-LIF residual block under-utilises
the latter.
\textbf{Candidate.}\;\;
Stacking two LIF blocks sequentially doubles the compute cost
without isolating the two frequency regimes.

\textbf{Our choice.}\;\;
The \DFRB{} (Fig.~\ref{fig:modules}\,(a)) parallelises a temporal
LIF path and a spatial path that operates on a $2\!\times\!$
PixelShuffle-downsampled representation:
\begin{align}
\mathbf{x}_h^{(1)} &\;=\; \mathrm{LIF}_1(\mathbf{x}),\quad
\mathbf{x}_l^{(1)} \;=\; \mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}_h^{(1)}, \\
\mathbf{x}_h^{(2)} &\;=\; \mathrm{PSLIF}_2\!\big(\mathrm{TDBN}(\mathrm{Conv}_1(\mathbf{x}_h^{(1)},\mathbf{x}_l^{(1)}))\big),\\
\mathbf{x}_{\mathrm{cross}} &\;=\; \sigma(g)\,\mathbf{x}_h^{(1)}\!\odot\!\mathbf{x}_h^{(2)},
\label{eq:dfrb-cross}
\end{align}
where $\mathrm{LIF}_1$ uses $\FQS{}$, $\mathrm{PSLIF}_2$ first applies
PixelShuffle-downsample and then LIF on the rearranged tensor (so
that spatially-adjacent pixels become temporally-adjacent in the
LIF state), and $\sigma(g)$ is a learnable scalar gate initialised
to $0$ so that the cross-scale path opens gradually during training.
The two paths are combined through learnable scales
$(\alpha_h^{(\ell)},\alpha_l^{(\ell)})\!\in\!\R^2$ for $\ell\!\in\!\{1,2\}$
and re-merged with a residual shortcut (Fig.~\ref{fig:modules}\,(a)).
\textbf{Improvement.}\;\;
Removing the spatial path collapses \DFRB{} to a single-LIF block;
in Section~\ref{sec:exp-ablation} this ablation costs $0.52$\,dB
PSNR.

\subsubsection{\SHAM{}: Spectral-Hybrid Attention Module}\label{sec:framework-sham}

\textbf{Problem.}\;\;
Conventional channel-attention modules~\cite{ref:cbam} recalibrate
features along the channel axis only, ignoring the temporal axis
$T$ that exists exclusively in spiking networks and the spatial
frequency axis that carries cloud texture. \textbf{Candidate.}\;\;
Separate temporal-only or spatial-only attention each handles one
dimension but loses the cross-dimension synergy.

\textbf{Our choice.}\;\;
\SHAM{} (Fig.~\ref{fig:modules}\,(c)) jointly recalibrates two
complementary axes. Given the spike-train feature volume
$\mathbf{X}\!\in\!\R^{T\times C\times H\times W}$:
\paragraph{(i) Temporal Amplitude (TA) branch.}
A spatially-pooled vector
$\mathbf{a}\!\in\!\R^T$ is fed through a Linear$(T\!\to\!T)$
followed by sigmoid to produce per-step weights
$\mathbf{w}_T\!\in\!\R^T$, which are broadcast back over $C\!\times\!H\!\times\!W$.
\paragraph{(ii) DCT-Spatial branch.}
The temporal mean
$\bar{\mathbf{X}}\!\in\!\R^{C\times H\times W}$ is mapped to its
magnitude spectrum via 2-D real FFT,
$|\mathcal{F}(\bar{\mathbf{X}})|$. A $1\!\times\!1$-Conv MLP
modulates the magnitude (preserving the phase), the inverse FFT
returns to the spatial domain, and a $7\!\times\!7$ convolution
emits the spatial attention mask
$\mathbf{w}_S\!\in\!\R^{1\times H\times W}$.
The two are fused as
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{SHAM}(\mathbf{X}) \;=\;
\mathbf{X}\;+\;\sigma(\eta)\!\cdot\!
\big(\mathbf{w}_T\!\otimes\!\mathbf{w}_S\!\otimes\!\mathbf{X}\big),
\label{eq:sham}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma(\eta)$ is a residual gate initialised to $0$, so the
module starts as identity and never destabilises a partly-trained
network. \textbf{Improvement.}\;\;
Removing \SHAM{} costs the largest single-component PSNR drop in
our ablation ($-0.60$\,dB on CR1, Section~\ref{sec:exp-ablation}),
indicating that frequency-aware attention---rather than parameter
count---is the principal source of \OrbitVLIF's quality advantage
over the parameter-matched ANN baseline.

\subsubsection{\AGFM{}: Adaptive Gated Fusion Module}\label{sec:framework-agfm}

\textbf{Problem.}\;\;
Standard U-Net skip connections fuse the encoder feature
$\mathbf{e}$ and the upsampled decoder feature $\mathbf{d}$ either
by addition or by concatenation, applying the \emph{same} fusion
weight at every spatial location. For cloud removal this is
sub-optimal: where the cloud is thin, the encoder features are
reliable and should dominate, but where the cloud is thick the
encoder features themselves are corrupted and the decoder
prediction should dominate. \textbf{Candidate.}\;\;A single
learnable scalar weight cannot adapt per-pixel to spatially
non-uniform occlusion. \textbf{Our choice.}\;\;\AGFM{} computes a
per-pixel sigmoid gate from the concatenated features:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{f} \;=\;
\boldsymbol{\gamma}\!\odot\!\mathbf{d} \;+\;
(\mathbf{1}\!-\!\boldsymbol{\gamma})\!\odot\!\mathbf{e},\quad
\boldsymbol{\gamma}\;=\;\sigma\!\big(\mathrm{Conv}_{1\!\times\!1}([\mathbf{d};\,\mathbf{e}])\big),
\label{eq:agfm}
\end{equation}
where $\boldsymbol{\gamma}\!\in\!\R^{C\times H\times W}$. The gate
is therefore content-adaptive: at thick-cloud pixels
$\boldsymbol{\gamma}\!\to\!1$ favours the decoder, while at
clean-pixel locations $\boldsymbol{\gamma}\!\to\!0$ preserves the
encoder detail.

\subsubsection{\SSHB{}: Spectro-temporal Spike Hyper-Block}\label{sec:framework-sshb}

The shallow level $\ell\!=\!0$ operates at full $H\!\times\!W$
resolution where the spike tensor still carries fine-grained spatial
detail and pixel-resolution texture cues critical for cloud
removal. \textbf{Problem.}\;\;A plain \DFRB{} at this stage
performs convolution at full resolution but lacks (i) a global
spatial mixer to align repetitive cloud patterns with the
underlying scene structure, and (ii) a mechanism to expand the
short $T\!=\!4$ time axis into a richer temporal context that the
sparse $\FQS$-quantized LIF (jointly denoted $\mathrm{VLIF}$) can
exploit. \textbf{Candidate.}\;\;A frequency-domain MLP, building on
\textsc{FreMLP}~\cite{ref:darkir}, provides $\mathcal{O}(N\log N)$
global spatial mixing, but operates only on the spatial axis and
ignores the temporal one.
\textbf{Our choice.}\;\;The \SSHB{} composes a temporal-expansion
front-end with a frequency-domain back-end:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{SSHB}(\mathbf{X}) \;=\;
\mathrm{FSE}\!\circ\!\mathrm{TCS}\!\circ\!
\big(\textsl{spike-conv refine}\big)\!\circ\!
\mathrm{TCAM}\!\circ\!\mathrm{VLIF}\!\circ\!
\mathrm{PU}_{2}\!\circ\!\mathrm{DFRB}(\mathbf{X}),
\label{eq:sshb}
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{PU}_{2}$ is a $2\!\times\!$ PixelUnshuffle that
trades $H\!\times\!W$ spatial extent for a $\times 4$ temporal
expansion ($T\!=\!4\!\to\!16$) so that the $\mathrm{TCAM}$ head can
recalibrate over a richer temporal context, the spike-conv refine
stage runs two $\mathrm{VLIF}$\!--\,$\mathrm{Conv}$\!--\,$\mathrm{TDBN}$\!--\,$\mathrm{TCS}$
sub-blocks at the reduced resolution before bilinear upsampling
back to $H\!\times\!W$, and the final $\mathrm{FSE}$ MLP modulates
the 2-D FFT magnitude (preserving phase) for global
$\mathcal{O}(N\log N)$ mixing. The \SSHB{} is used at the wide
outer level---once in the encoder and twice in the
decoder---where the absolute energy budget per pixel is highest;
the per-block SOP cost is offset by the temporal sparsity that
$\mathrm{VLIF}$ maintains at full resolution
(Section~\ref{sec:exp-energy}).

\subsection{Loss Function}\label{sec:framework-loss}

We train end-to-end with a composite Charbonnier-plus-SSIM
objective inherited from prior SNN image-restoration
work~\cite{ref:esdnet}:
\begin{equation}
\ell\big(\hat{\mathbf{y}},\,\mathbf{y}\big) \;=\;
\frac{1}{|\Omega|}\!\sum_{p\in\Omega}\!
\sqrt{(\hat{\mathbf{y}}_p\!-\!\mathbf{y}_p)^2 + \varepsilon^2}
\;+\;\lambda\,\big(1 - \mathrm{SSIM}(\hat{\mathbf{y}},\mathbf{y})\big),
\label{eq:loss}
\end{equation}
with $\varepsilon\!=\!10^{-3}$ and $\lambda\!=\!0.1$. The
Charbonnier term (a smooth $L_1$ surrogate) preserves the
pixel-level $L$-smoothness assumption underlying any SGD-based
federated convergence guarantee; the SSIM term acts as a
perceptual regulariser.

\subsection{Decentralized Federated Training Pipeline}\label{sec:framework-fl}

We instantiate the four-step procedure of
Section~\ref{sec:sysmodel-procedure} with two design choices that
are jointly tuned to the source-level Dirichlet regime: (i)
\textsc{FedBN} for BN handling, and (ii) Gossip averaging for
inter-plane mixing.

\subsubsection*{Why \textsc{FedBN}}
Under source-level Dirichlet $\alpha\!=\!0.1$, $60\,\%$ of the
$50$ satellites hold a single source at the floor of five tiles
(Section~\ref{sec:sysmodel-partition}). The per-satellite BN running statistics
$(\mu_{i,k},\sigma^2_{i,k})$ are therefore strongly source-conditioned.
Averaging these statistics globally---as in vanilla
\textsc{FedAvg}---would force a single neuron to encode the
union of two distinct cloud-density distributions, dragging both
modes towards a degraded average. \textsc{FedBN}~\cite{ref:fedbn}
keeps the BN affine and running statistics \emph{local} to each
satellite while still averaging convolutional weights and biases,
restoring the per-source calibration freedom needed for stable
SNN spike statistics.

\subsubsection*{Why Gossip averaging}
Among the standard inter-plane aggregation
options---centralised AllReduce, Gossip
averaging~\cite{ref:gossip1,ref:gossip2}, and RelaySum~\cite{ref:relaysum}---we
adopt single-step Gossip with a doubly-stochastic mixing matrix
$\mathbf{W}$ (eq.~\ref{eq:gossip}). The choice is motivated by two
properties suited to satellite deployment: (i) Gossip is
\emph{decentralised}---no plane needs to act as a coordinator,
removing a single point of failure; (ii) per-round inter-plane
bandwidth is $\mathcal{O}(N\!\cdot\!d)$, independent of the
topology diameter. The RelaySum
analysis~\cite{ref:relaysum} bounds federated SGD convergence by a
$\zeta^2\!\cdot\!\tau$ term that dominates only when the
inter-client gradient dissimilarity $\zeta^2$ is large; for
source-level Dirichlet over $S\!=\!2$ sources with the dense
Charbonnier loss~\eqref{eq:loss}, $\zeta^2$ is structurally smaller
than for label-Dirichlet over many classes, placing all three
schemes in the same asymptotic order. Under this regime, Gossip's
decentralisation property dictates the choice; we therefore focus
on Gossip and do not run a head-to-head head against AllReduce or
RelaySum in this work.

\subsubsection*{Communication-cost accounting}
The conv subset of \OrbitVLIF{} that participates in inter-plane
Gossip is $d_\textsc{conv}\!\approx\!2.25$\,M float-32 parameters
($\sim 9$\,MB) after excluding the local BN affine and running
statistics. The aggregate per-round inter-plane traffic, summed
over all active edges of the $5$-plane chain topology, is
empirically $\sim 74$\,MB (full state-dict transmissions exchanged
along the chain); over $T\!=\!200$ rounds the cumulative cross-plane
traffic is therefore $\sim 15$\,GB, dominated by Gossip rather
than the intra-plane ring AllReduce that operates on the
quasi-stationary high-bandwidth channels. Per-round numbers are
recorded in Section~\ref{sec:exp-federated} (Table~III).

The complete \OrbitVLIF{} decentralised training procedure is
summarised in Algorithm~\ref{alg:orbitvlif}.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{\OrbitVLIF{} Decentralised Training}\label{alg:orbitvlif}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
  \REQUIRE Initial weights $\mathbf{x}^{(0)}$; planes $\mathcal{N}$, satellites
           $\mathcal{K}_i$; mixing matrix $\mathbf{W}\!\in\!\R^{N\!\times\!N}$;
           local epochs $E$; global rounds $T$; learning rate
           schedule $\{\eta_t\}$; local datasets $\{\D_{i,k}\}$
  \FOR{$t = 0, 1, \ldots, T-1$}
    \STATE \textbf{Step~S1 — local update (parallel over satellites).}
    \FORALL{$(i,k)\in\mathcal{N}\!\times\!\mathcal{K}_i$ in parallel}
      \STATE $\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,0)} \!\leftarrow\! \mathbf{x}^{(t)}_i$
      \FOR{$e = 0, \ldots, E\!-\!1$}
        \STATE $\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,e+1)} \!\leftarrow\!
                \mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,e)}\!-\!\eta_t\nabla\widetilde f_{i,k}\!\big(\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,e)}\big)$
      \ENDFOR
    \ENDFOR
    \STATE \textbf{Step~S2 — intra-plane ring AllReduce.}
    \FOR{$i\!\in\!\mathcal{N}$ in parallel}
      \STATE $\mathbf{x}_i^{(t+\tfrac12)}\!\leftarrow\!\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k}\!\mathbf{x}_{i,k}^{(t,E)}$
    \ENDFOR
    \STATE \textbf{Step~S3 — inter-plane Gossip (FedBN: conv only).}
    \FOR{$i\!\in\!\mathcal{N}$}
      \STATE $\mathbf{x}_i^{(t+1)}\!\leftarrow\!
              \sum_{j}\!W_{ij}\,\mathbf{x}_{j,\,\textsc{conv}}^{(t+\tfrac12)}\;\cup\;
              \mathbf{x}_{i,\,\textsc{bn}}^{(t+\tfrac12)}$ \COMMENT{BN local}
    \ENDFOR
    \STATE \textbf{Step~S4 — broadcast $\mathbf{x}_i^{(t+1)}$ to every $s_{i,k}$ in plane $i$.}
  \ENDFOR
  \RETURN $\{\mathbf{x}_i^{(T)}\}_{i\in\mathcal{N}}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\section{Experiments}\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}\label{sec:exp-setup}

\textbf{Datasets.}\;\;We use the CUHK-CR
benchmark~\cite{ref:cuhkcr} comprising two paired
\mbox{(cloudy,\,clear)} sets at $0.5$\,m ground sampling distance:
\textbf{CUHK-CR1} (thin clouds, $534$ train / $134$ test) and
\textbf{CUHK-CR2} (thick clouds, $448$ train / $111$ test).
Centralised runs train and evaluate on each split independently.
Federated runs use the joint pool ($982$ train / $245$ test) under
the source-level Dirichlet partition of
Section~\ref{sec:sysmodel-partition}.

\textbf{Constellation.}\;\;A $5\!\times\!10\!=\!50$ Walker-Star
configuration with a fixed inter-plane chain topology, matching the
FLSNN setup~\cite{ref:flsnn}.

\textbf{Model.}\;\;\OrbitVLIF{} with channel width $C\!=\!24$,
encoder block counts $(2,2,4)$, decoder $(2,2,2)$, $T\!=\!4$,
$V_\textrm{th}\!=\!0.15$, $\FQS$ activation, and TDBN with
$\gamma_{\rm init}\!=\!\alpha_{\rm bn}V_\textrm{th}$ following the
spiking-BN initialisation of~\cite{ref:flsnn}. Total parameters
$d\!\approx\!2.30$\,M.

\textbf{Training.}\;\;AdamW with $\eta_0\!=\!10^{-3}$ cosine-annealed
to $10^{-7}$, $3$-epoch warmup, gradient-norm clip $1.0$. Centralised
runs use batch size $8$ with $600$ epochs ($\OrbitVLIF$ on CR1/CR2)
or $400$ epochs (PlainUNet on CR1/CR2); ablations use $300$ epochs.
Federated runs use batch size $4$ per satellite (the constraint
$\textsc{min}_\textrm{client}\!\geq\!\textsc{batch}$
under \texttt{drop\_last=True} dictates the
choice~\cite{ref:fedbn}) for $T\!=\!200$ rounds with $E\!=\!2$
local epochs per round. Synchronised geometric augmentation
(h-flip, v-flip, $\pm 90^\circ$ rotation) is applied identically
to (cloudy,\,clear) pairs; no colour jitter is used because radiance
has physical meaning.

\textbf{Evaluation.}\;\;Centre-patch ($64\!\times\!64$)
PSNR and SSIM averaged over the held-out test split. For per-plane
statistics under federated training, we report the
cross-image\,$\to$\,cross-plane mean.

\textbf{Baselines.}\;\;A \emph{PlainUNet} of equivalent capacity
($C\!=\!24$, encoder/decoder block counts identical to \OrbitVLIF,
plain BN+ReLU residual blocks, no \SHAM, no \SSHB, no spike
quantisation, $1.31$\,M parameters) isolates the contribution of
\OrbitVLIF's spiking + frequency-aware design. Federated experiments
additionally include an ANN-\OrbitVLIF{} variant (every LIF
replaced by ReLU; same skeleton, same parameter count) to isolate
the spike-vs-ANN gap on identical architecture.

\textbf{Hardware.}\;\;Two NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPUs ($24$\,GB each) on
isolated AutoDL containers; no inter-container synchronisation
(each container runs an independent experiment chain).

\subsection{Component Ablation}\label{sec:exp-ablation}

To isolate the contribution of each \OrbitVLIF{} building block, we
ablate three of the four blocks defined in
Section~\ref{sec:framework-arch} on CUHK-CR1 under matched training
( $300$ epochs, otherwise identical to the centralised setting):

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{B1: $-$\SHAM{}.}\;\;\SHAM{} (eq.~\ref{eq:sham}) is
        replaced by the identity, removing the joint
        temporal-amplitude / DCT-spatial recalibration.
  \item \textbf{B2: $-$DualPath.}\;\;The PixelShuffle-LIF spatial
        path of \DFRB{} (eqs.~\ref{eq:dfrb-cross}) is removed,
        collapsing each block to a single LIF residual unit; the
        $\sigma(g)$ cross-scale gate is also removed.
  \item \textbf{B3: $-$\FQS{}.}\;\;The $5$-level quantisation of
        eq.~\ref{eq:5qs-forward} is replaced by a binary
        $\{0,1\}$ spike. The surrogate gradient window is kept
        identical so that only the forward quantisation
        granularity changes.
\end{itemize}

\AGFM{} is not separately ablated because its sigmoid-gated form
degenerates to plain addition at $\boldsymbol{\gamma}\!=\!\tfrac12$ and
no surgery is needed in code.

high-frequency content otherwise absent from the temporal-LIF
path; \FQS{} contributes the smallest drop because the
$300$-epoch budget under-utilises its multi-level dynamic range
(membrane potentials require $\sim$50 epochs to warm up past
$V_\textrm{th}$ under the TDBN initialisation~\cite{ref:flsnn}).
With the full $600$-epoch schedule of A1 we expect this gap to
widen.

\textbf{Co-design evidence.}\;\;A striking observation is that two
of the three single-component ablations---B1 ($24.777$) and B2
($24.858$)---fall \emph{below} the PlainUNet baseline ($24.872$),
even though the ablated networks retain the rest of the
\OrbitVLIF{} architecture. Removing one component therefore breaks
\OrbitVLIF{} more than reverting entirely to a plain ANN. This is
strong evidence that \OrbitVLIF's blocks are co-designed: the
$+0.50$\,dB CR1 advantage of the full model is not the sum of
independently-additive component gains but a synergistic effect
that requires all four blocks to operate together. Only the B3
ablation, which keeps the architecture intact and only relaxes
the spike granularity, remains above PlainUNet---confirming that
$\FQS$ is a refinement rather than a load-bearing component.

\subsection{Federated Cloud-Removal Performance}\label{sec:exp-federated}

This section evaluates the central system contribution of this
work: the decentralized \textsc{FedBN}\,+\,\emph{Gossip} aggregation
pipeline of Algorithm~\ref{alg:orbitvlif}.  Unlike conventional
parameter-server federated training, our scheme runs entirely
between satellites via inter-/intra-plane links: intra-plane
ring-AllReduce within each of the five orbital planes, followed
by inter-plane Gossip averaging on a sparse contact graph
respecting LEO visibility windows.  We deploy three matched-protocol
backbones (\OrbitVLIF{}-SNN, \OrbitVLIF{}-ANN, and a plain ANN
U-Net baseline) on the $5\!\times\!10\!=\!50$-satellite
Walker-Star constellation, under source-level Dirichlet
$\alpha\!=\!0.1$ for $T\!=\!200$ rounds. All three share the
identical pipeline of Algorithm~\ref{alg:orbitvlif} and differ
only in the per-satellite network, isolating the effect of the
backbone from the aggregation algorithm. Two additional
\OrbitVLIF{}-SNN runs at $\alpha\!\in\!\{0.01,1.0\}$ probe
sensitivity to the non-IID strength.

\textbf{Note on F\_snn cells.}\;\;
The \OrbitVLIF{}-SNN federated runs at $\alpha\!\in\!\{0.1, 0.01,

already complete.

  \centering

  (blue, solid) at $77$\,fJ/SOP, the neuromorphic SOP cost used
  by FLSNN, ESDNet, and VLIF. Multipliers vs.\ ANN are annotated
  above each bar; dashed orange line marks the ANN reference

  forward hooks; only the pJ-per-operation constants are taken
  from the literature.}
  \label{fig:energy}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{fig9_per_layer_spike_rate.pdf}
  \caption{\OrbitVLIF{} per-layer LIF firing rate (fraction of
  non-zero outputs) on CUHK-CR1 test, measured through forward
  hooks on every LIF / $\FQS$ module. Binary LIF layers exhibit
  $20$\,--\,$70\,\%$ firing; $\FQS$-equipped layers (\textsc{lif\_node})
  show a higher non-zero floor because four of the five
  quantization levels are non-zero by construction.}
  \label{fig:firing-rate}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Methodology and disclosure.}\;\;Per-image inference energy
is \emph{estimated}, not hardware-measured. We hook every
\texttt{Conv2d} / \texttt{Linear} layer to count MACs and the
input non-zero rate $r_\ell$, and every LIF / $\FQS$ module to
record the output firing rate, on $32$ CR1 test patches at
$64\!\times\!64$. The MAC counts and per-layer rates are real
measurements; the per-operation pJ constants are taken from the
literature: $4.6$\,pJ/MAC for the ANN baseline (Horowitz, ISSCC
2014, 45\,nm CMOS) and $77$\,fJ/SOP for the SNN deployment
estimate, the constant adopted by FLSNN~\cite{ref:flsnn},
ESDNet~\cite{ref:esdnet}, and VLIF~\cite{ref:fsta-snn} for
neuromorphic / in-memory-computing substrates such as Loihi-2.
Hardware-measured wattage on actual neuromorphic platforms is
deferred to deployment validation
(Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}).

\textbf{Two distinct rates.}\;\;\OrbitVLIF{} exhibits two
complementary rates that we report separately. The
\emph{LIF firing rate} (Fig.~\ref{fig:firing-rate}) is the
fraction of non-zero outputs at LIF / $\FQS$ neurons themselves
and is the empirical witness that spike sparsity is real, not a
simulation artefact: across the LIF layers it ranges over
$20$\,--\,$70\,\%$. The \emph{effective non-zero MAC ratio}
$\bar{r}=0.826$, averaged across all conv layers, is what enters
the energy formula
$E_{\rm SNN}\!=\!\sum_\ell\mathrm{MAC}_\ell\,r_\ell\,e_{\rm op}$
and serves as the direct input to the dual bound below.

\textbf{Total energy bounds.}\;\;Figure~\ref{fig:energy} reports
per-image bounds. The ANN baseline at $4.6$\,pJ/MAC over the
$4.51$\,GMAC inference consumes $20.75$\,mJ. \OrbitVLIF{} spans a
dual bound:\;a \emph{deployment-target lower} of $0.287$\,mJ
($72.3\!\times\!$ reduction) at $77$\,fJ/SOP on neuromorphic
hardware, and a \emph{conservative upper} of $17.14$\,mJ
($1.21\!\times\!$ reduction) that charges every non-zero MAC at
the full $4.6$\,pJ ANN rate. The dual-bound bracket honestly
reflects the deployment uncertainty: $1.21\!\times\!$ is achieved
even without any neuromorphic hardware, while $72\!\times\!$
requires a substrate that supports the full SOP / spike abstraction
(Loihi-2, Speck, Akida).

\textbf{Energy is data-dependent in principle.}\;\;Comparing
CR1 and CR2 inferences on the same architecture (A1 ckpt vs.\ A2
ckpt) we measure effective non-zero MAC ratios of $82.6\,\%$ and
$82.3\,\%$ respectively---differing by only $0.3$ percentage
points, yielding indistinguishable $E_{\rm SNN}$ at two decimal
places. Two mechanisms stabilise this rate across cloud-density
distributions: (i) TDBN with $\gamma_{\rm init}=\alpha_{\rm bn}V_{\rm th}$
normalises activation magnitudes to a fixed range; (ii) the
$\FQS$ four-of-five non-zero floor enforces a structural lower
bound on the firing rate at LIF outputs. We therefore do
\emph{not} claim data-independence of the energy estimate, only
that the two CUHK-CR distributions are statistically close enough
to yield indistinguishable bounds.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented \OrbitVLIF, the first end-to-end decentralized
federated learning system for satellite-onboard SNN-based optical
cloud removal. The central technical contribution is the
\textsc{FedBN}\,+\,\emph{Gossip} aggregation pipeline that
exchanges model weights entirely between satellites along
intra-plane ring and inter-plane visibility links, dispensing with
a parameter server and respecting LEO contact windows. The model
is offered in two configurations on a shared dataflow: a $14$M
\emph{full-capacity} variant that reaches SOTA-class quality on
CUHK-CR1\,/\,CR2 (Section~\ref{sec:exp-fullsota}), and a $2$M
\emph{on-orbit deployment} variant used in the federated and
energy experiments. The U-Net itself integrates a dual-frequency
residual block, a spectral-hybrid attention module, a $5$-level
quantized spike activation, an adaptive gated skip-fusion, and an
outer-stage spectro-temporal hyper block---components adapted from
prior SNN image-restoration work and combined for the
cloud-removal task.

Empirically, the deployment variant attains
$25.37$\,dB\,/\,$0.7728$ PSNR\,/\,SSIM on CUHK-CR1
($+0.50$\,dB over a parameter-matched ANN U-Net at
$0.57\!\times\!$ the parameter count) and $23.20$\,dB on
CUHK-CR2; per-image inference energy is \emph{estimated} as
$1.21\!\times$\,--\,$72\!\times$ lower than the ANN baseline of
$20.75$\,mJ under standard literature pJ-per-operation
constants. Component ablations demonstrate that the four
building blocks form a co-designed whole: removing \SHAM{} or the
dual-frequency \DFRB{} path drops PSNR \emph{below} the plain ANN
baseline.

Four directions extend this work.
(i) Closing the federated-vs-centralised gap (currently
$2.86$\,dB for PlainUNet under $\alpha\!=\!0.1$) via partial
participation~\cite{ref:fedavg-partial} or local
momentum~\cite{ref:scaffold}, in particular under the
\OrbitVLIF{}-SNN backbone whose $\alpha$-sensitivity runs are
deferred to the extended version.
(ii) Real-orbit validation on neuromorphic substrates such as
Loihi-2 / Speck would replace the analytic energy bracket with
measured power draw.
(iii) Multi-source generalisation beyond
CUHK-CR1\,+\,CR2 (e.g., Sentinel-2 vs.\ Landsat\,8) would exercise
larger source-level Dirichlet partitions with $S\!\geq\!3$.
(iv) Inter-plane mixing matrix learning---rather than the
fixed-chain $\mathbf{W}$ used here---could exploit orbital
geometry to minimise convergence time.
